Money

15 July 2015

"I love money. I love everything about it. I bought some pretty good stuff. Got me a $300 pair of socks. Got a fur sink. An electric dog polisher. A gasoline powered turtleneck sweater. And, of course, I bought some dumb stuff, too." –Steve Martin

As an undergraduate student, most of the decisions I make are to optimize my chances of financial stability in the future . I enjoy learning and I am a computer science major who is pretty confident about my GPA. My performance comes from my love of the discipline; however, having a lucrative profession and living an affluent lifestyle is what I expect to be the result of my hard work.

A constant thought I have is "if I am making a lot of money will I not be comfortable and happy that I can take care of myself"? The feeling of buying things I want with complete financial security would make me feel very good. Is this not happiness? Now I know most people will say “no, this is not”, but this is what I currently believe. Maybe I am not mature enough to tell the difference between happiness and financial success. Or perhaps there is a blurred line between happiness and a lucrative lifestyle. What do you think?
